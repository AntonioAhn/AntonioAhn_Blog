Introduction

I’m a postdoctoral scientist at the Peter MacCallum Cancer centre as a member of the Goel lab.

I am dual trained in both wet lab techniques (molecular and cellular biology) and bioinformatics. I started as a wet lab biologist working in a diagnostics lab, before then specialising in a range of cell and molecular-based assays during my post-graduate studies. Over the course of my PhD, I came to appreciate the importance of “big data” analysis in modern cancer research and decided to learn cancer bioinformatics, focusing on analysis of transcriptomic and epigenomic data. I recently moved to the Peter MacCallum Cancer Centre in Melbourne, where my primary research interest is in understanding how a small fraction of tumor cells develop resistance to anti-cancer therapies by entering a distinct phenotypic state termed therapy-induced senescence. It is astonishing how these cells quickly adapt to the cytotoxicity of therapy, and I am excited about integrating epigenetics, bioinformatics, and wet-lab techniques to find out how these cells behave and to identify new drugs that can kill them.
